Covenant Requirements

Regulatory Considerations
Several regulatory requirements must be satisfied prior to or shortly after financial close. Environmental permits for the new production line are pending and must be secured before initial drawdown. The borrower is also required to renew its occupational safety compliance certification within the next six months.
Market Conditions / Competitive Landscape
The U.S. mid-market manufacturing sector is currently experiencing moderate growth, supported by the broader trend of reshoring and increased domestic investment in supply chain resilience. However, rising input costs, particularly for steel and aluminium, present a near-term challenge to margin stability.
Additional Notes
The private equity sponsor intends to strengthen management by bringing in experienced industry executives within the first six months following transaction close. Loan documentation is expected to include a step-up in margin in the event of a DSCR breach, providing an additional incentive for compliance.

Recommendation
Based on our comprehensive analysis, we recommend APPROVAL of the $25M senior credit facility. The company demonstrates strong fundamentals, conservative leverage, and multiple paths to debt service. While certain risks exist around customer concentration and capex covenants, these are manageable and well-mitigated.
Conditions of Approval
  1. Environmental permit for new production line must be obtained prior to first drawdown.
  2. Borrower to provide updated capex budget within 30 days of closing.
  3. Key-man insurance policy on founder-CEO to be in place at closing.
  4. Quarterly DSCR and leverage covenant compliance certificates required.
  5. No additional unsecured borrowing above $2M without lender consent in the first 18 months.
